Chapter 2                                                        Customer Service

 

 

2.  The proposal stage

The agent has to support the customer in filling out the proposal for insurance. By understanding the importance of the question as the insured is required to take responsibility for the statements made therein. which  can jeopardize the customer’s claim.

 

3.    Acceptance stage

a)   Cover note (chapter 5)

It is the agent’s responsibility to ensure that the cover note is issued promptly by the company, where applicable, to the insured.

 

b)  Delivery of the policy document

If company rules permit a policy document being delivered in person, it may be a good idea to collect it and present the document to the customer.

If the policy is being sent directly by mail, it is an opportunity to visit the customer and explain policy provisions, and the policy holder’s rights and privileges that the customer can avail of.

The agent can ask for the names and particulars of other individuals he knows who can possibly benefit from the agent’s services. If the client can himself contact these people and introduce the agent to them, it would mean a great breakthrough in business.

 

c)  Policy renewal

Here the goodwill and trust created by the agent and the company gets tested, ensures high persistency and retention of customers.

As a matter of courtesy and decidedly a healthy business practice, insurers issue a “Renewal Notice” one month in advance of the date of expiry, inviting renewal of the policy. The agent needs to be in touch with the customer well before the renewal due date.

 

4.  The claim stage

It is the duty of the agent to ensure that the incident giving rise to the claim is immediately informed to the insurer, customer is guided to complete all formalities and  assists in all the investigations that may need to be done to assess the loss.
