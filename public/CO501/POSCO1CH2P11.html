Chapter 2                                                 Indian Insurance Market

 

IRDAI: Main Bodies

Insurance Advisory Committee

As per the power given by the Central Government under Section 25 of the Act, the Authority has appointed Insurance Advisory Committee to advise the Authority on matters relating to the making of the regulations consistent with the Act and Rules to carry out the purposes of the Act.

The Chairperson and the members of the Authority are the ex-officio members of the Insurance Advisory Committee. The committee consists not more than 25 members excluding ex-officio members

 

General  & Life Insurance Councils

The General  & the Life Insurance Councils comprising Public and Private sector insurers are the Self – Regulating Organizations and have set up their Secretariat at Mumbai. The following are its functions as an Industry Association.

-        Liaise with the Government and IRDAI to give the feedback of the industry on various issues to the Regulator.

-        Address market conduct issues.

 

Functions of Executive Committee (General & Life Councils)

The following are the main functions of the Executive Committees of the General and Life Insurance Councils:

a.    Aid, advise and assist insurers carrying on General / Life Insurance business in,

a.    setting up standards of conduct and sound practice

b.    rendering efficient service to life insurance policy holders

b.    Render advice to the IRDAI in controlling the expenses of insurers in respect of their general / life insurance business.

c.    Bring to notice of the IRDAI any case of an insurer acting prejudicial to the interests of the policyholders.

d.    Act in any matter incidental or ancillary to any of the matters specified to clauses (a) to (c) above as may be notified by the General and Life Insurance Councils in the Gazette of India, with the approval of the IRDAI.
