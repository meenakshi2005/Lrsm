Chapter 3                                 Principles and Practice of Insurance

 

1- Offer [Proposal ] and Acceptance

Insurance is a contract which is governed by the Indian Contract Act 1874. Proposal is the basis of the contract of insurance.

 

Offer:

Proposal [Offer] is a printed application for taking an insurance Policy. When an insured approaches insurance company to insure his life or his property he is required to fill up a form called Proposal form. In proposal form the questions are asked, depending on type of insurance sought for.

 

Examples:

a) In case insurance of life, generally question about age, gender, health conditions, occupation, income etc are asked.

b) In case of property insurance- say for insurance of a house- questions related to type of construction, age of the building, distance from river etc are asked.

 

Acceptance:

Depending  upon the details given in the proposal insurer decides about acceptance or rejection of the proposal. If they accept the proposal as it is, it is called “accepted as proposed”. Unless accepted by the insurer, proposal becomes infructuous.

In many cases insurers may be willing to accept the proposal but for lesser covers or modified terms.

 

e.g. If Shyam approached an insurance company to insure his life against death & disability, the insurer may not be willing to grant disability cover but may be prepared to grant only death cover. In this case the proposal is not accepted but what is done by the insurance company is called issue of a “Counter Offer”

When a counter offer is made, the proposer has to decide whether he will accept the counter offer or not. If he accepts, then it amount to acceptance.
