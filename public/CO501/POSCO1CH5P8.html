Chapter 5                                                                  Miscellaneous

 

2. Don’ts for POS Person-

No POS PERSON shall :

i. Solicit or procure insurance business without being appointed to act as such by the insurer.

ii. Induce the prospect to omit any material information in the proposal form.

iii. Induce the prospect to submit wrong information in the proposal form or documents submitted to the insurer for acceptance of the proposal

iv. Resort to multilevel marketing for soliciting and procuring insurance policies and/or induct any prospect/policyholder into a multilevel level marketing scheme.

v. Behave in a discourteous manner with the prospect;

vi. Interfere with any proposal introduced by any other intermediary;

vii. Offer different rates, advantages, terms and conditions other those offered by his insurer;

viii. Demand or receive a share of proceeds from the beneficiary under an insurance contract;

ix.  Force a policyholder to terminate the existing policy and to effect a new policy from him

     within three years from the date of such termination of the earlier policy;

x. Apply for fresh appointment to act as an insurance agent, if his appointment was earlier cancelled by the designated official, and a period of five years has not elapsed form the date of such cancellation;

xi. Become or remain a director of any insurer.

 

3.Every POS PERSON shall, with a view to conserve the insurance business already procured through him, make every attempt to ensure remittance of the premiums by the policyholders within the stipulated time, by giving notice to the policyholder orally and in writing;

 

4. Acting as a POS PERSO in violation of the provision of the Insurance Act, 1938 and Regulation will attract a penalty prescribed by the IRDAI and any insurer or any person acting on behalf of an insurer, who appoints any person as a POS PERSON not permitted to act as such or transact any insurance business in India through any such person shall be liable to penalty prescribed by the IRDAI..

 

5. The insurer shall be responsible for all acts and omission of its POS PERSON including violation of code of conduct specified under these Regulation, and shall be liable to a penalty as prescribed by the IRDAI..
